Curved population geometry shapes the stability of ambiguous natural texture representations in mouse V1
Visual textures are often used to probe neural visual processing in neuroscientific research.  Natural textures are used firstly due to their prevalence within the natural environment (Balaños et al., 2022). Furthermore, they are more easily synthesized and parameterized than conventional stimuli, allowing for more precise study (Victor et al,. 2017). 
Textures are thought to be used by the brain to determine information about a visual scene in the early parts of the visual system. These textures provide information about a visual scene in two distinct manners: They provide information about (1) the reflectance, the intrinsic spatial characteristics of a surface, and (2) the surface normal of an object, the extrinsic 3D orientation of that surface relative to the observer (Leung and Malik, 2001). In the primary visual cortex (V1), these features are encoded by the collective activity of neuronal populations. According to a recent framework, these encoded textures can be thought of as a manifold in a highly dimensional neural-state space (Chung and Abbot, 2021).
